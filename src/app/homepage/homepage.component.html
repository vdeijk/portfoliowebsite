About Rick

Rick van der Eijk is a self-taught, freelance, full-stack developer with a passion for how development and design interact. He has worked for enterprises such as Unilever, the Dutch government, and IBM, but also for startups and other small businesses. He thrives in environments where enthusiasm and dedication are rewarded, and where good work ethic can make a difference.

In 2015-2016, for ZZF, a small but well-known Dutch charity (ZZF), Rick redesigned a CRM system from the ground up, enabling the organization to store its many contacts in a more organized fashion.

Rick started his journey to become a full stack developer in January 2020. However, barely after, Covid struck. Rather than giving up, he doubled down, regularly putting in over 80 hours per week, so he could both pay his bills and make progress on his studies. His website (https://portfolio-rick-van-der-eijk.web.app/) was built during that time, as well as several portfolio projects that can be found on there.

In March 2021, Rick was hired by IBM as a frontend developer. During the rest of the year (April to December 2021), he put in over 300 study hours, earning him the status of Champion Learner Gold, which only 5 percent of IBM-ers attain. Within his 18 months at IBM, Rick also promoted twice, earning him special commendation for his technical skills.

Because of Rick’s background in sales, his communication skills are excellent. For example, at the Unilever project, Rick knew that the chosen tech stack wasn’t going to work well, even though it had more or less been decided before he arrived. Due to this risk, Rick persuaded fellow team members, some of them many levels above his own, to alter course. This led to a result that could otherwise never have been attained. Afterwards, several potential customers praised the frontend that Rick had built.

Rick is always looking for new challenges to overcome, which is why he decided to learn Unity in August 2021. Fascinatingly, his game idea involved complex 3D vector math and physics, even though he has never had formal technical education of any kind. His project, Mars Commander, is fully playable and currently in alpha state: https://vdeijk.itch.io/mars-commander

Currently, Rick works as a freelancer for Reflectlab in Eindhoven. Contact him for more info about this assignment, or for opportunities to work with him.
